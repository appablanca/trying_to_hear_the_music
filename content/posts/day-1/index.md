Today I started this blog.

I began my Master’s at KIT on 20.04.2026. Since then, I’ve been struggling with the content of my courses:

- Advanced Data Structures  
- Parameterized Algorithms  
- Algorithm Engineering  
- Fine-Grained Complexity Theory and Algorithms  
- Seminar: Fine-Grained Complexity Theory and Algorithms  
- Seminar: Current Trends in Theoretical Computer Science  

It might be too early to worry about my understanding, but I’m not used to *not* understanding what’s going on in lectures. During my Bachelor’s, I would usually follow everything in real time—sometimes even staying ahead.

I expected the Master’s to be harder, but not to **this extent**.

Another challenge is the shift in perspective. In my Bachelor’s in Computer Engineering, most courses revolved around implementation. Now, the focus feels much more theoretical—centered on complexity theory, abstractions, and concepts like NP-hardness.

This blog will document my attempt to adapt to this new way of thinking about algorithms. I plan to write about what I encounter in lectures, what I understand, and what I don’t. I’ll also cover the extra material I need to study outside of coursework to truly grasp the topics.

Ideally, I’ll update this blog daily—but no promises.
